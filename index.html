Homework Batch #1
📚 Being first to the footy

Being first to the footy and grabbing it cleanly is one of the most important skills in footy. The best AFL footballers are the ones that can pick the ball up cleanly off the ground first go without any fumbling. They do this while they are on the move, and sometimes running at full pace which means they are harder to catch and tackle. This is the number 1 skill to try to master in Juniors.

📚 Patrick Dangerfield : Fast and clean with the footy

Patrick Dangerfield from Geelong is one of the best in the AFL at winning clearances for his team. He runs hard through the contest, uses his body and his strength to attack the ball, then grabs it cleanly first go and either sprints out, or gets the footy to a team mate quickly.

📚 It takes a whole team effort

In footy the real efforts that make a team successful aren't the flashy things like the big marks and big goals. It's much more about the small things that are harder to notice. Like everyone RUNNING HARD to be first and to win ball, to create options for your team-mates, to back-up, to shepherd or to run back hard in defence. It's putting 100% COMMITMENT into each contest to scramble, bump, block, smother, tackle and put PRESSURE on to win the ball for our team. It's TALKING to your team mates to let them know if they are HOT! or if they are CLEAR! A lot of these things are done when you don't even have the ball in your hand, and it's all of these things when added together that create the real success for the team overall.
